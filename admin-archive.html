Internal Audit Review

AUDIT REF: IA-2037-11
DEPARTMENT: Public Records Changes
REVIEW PERIOD: Q1 2038

Audit Scope:
This review examined administrative actions taken on select Missing Person records
within the Public Records database, with emphasis on post-publication alterations,
access control changes, and inter-departmental data consistency.

Methodology:
The audit evaluated system logs, authorization trails, and revision histories
associated with affected records. No interviews were conducted.

Findings:
• Identifying fields were modified following initial record publication
• Case notes and descriptive metadata were reclassified and relocated
• Public-facing visibility was reduced across civilian access systems
• No corresponding case closure documentation was located

Authorization Basis:
Changes were executed under standing administrative discretion granted to
Executive Continuity and Records Oversight personnel for data integrity
management and continuity-risk mitigation.

No individual authorizing official was recorded in the system logs.
All actions fell within existing permission thresholds at time of execution.

Assessment:
The modifications materially altered public presentation of the affected records
without triggering standard escalation or review requirements.

Conclusion:
Actions were determined to be compliant with internal policy as written.
Current policy does not require retrospective correction.

Final Status: CLOSED

SUBJECT: Record Consistency Review — Action Requested

During routine continuity verification, a variance was identified between
identity markers associated with a Missing Person record currently indexed
across Public Safety, Health Services, and Civilian Records systems.

Specifically, timeline alignment checks are producing repeat conflicts that
cannot be resolved through automated reconciliation.

To prevent further propagation of inconsistent data across downstream systems,
we recommend the following interim action:

• Reduce public-facing visibility of the affected record
• Limit cross-departmental indexing pending resolution

This action is intended as a containment measure only and should not be
interpreted as case closure or status determination.

permissions.
